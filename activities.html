Research

Independent Research Agenda

The Lab produces independent, social science-based research on the actors, institutions, norms, and processes shaping digital governance globally. Our research spans current and emerging challenges in the digital governance landscape.

01

Digital Sovereignty

How do states assert control over digital infrastructure, data, and platforms — and what are the international implications of competing sovereignty claims? This strand examines the political economy of digital independence, data localisation policies, and the sovereignty discourse in multilateral negotiations.

Data Localisation Internet Fragmentation Jurisdictional Conflicts
02

AI Governance

Mapping the rapidly proliferating landscape of AI governance initiatives — national strategies, regional frameworks, and multilateral processes — and assessing their coherence, inclusivity, and effectiveness. Particular attention to the ITU AI for Good process and emerging UN frameworks.

UN AI Governance Regulatory Approaches Global South
03

Data Flows & Digital Standards

Examining the governance of cross-border data flows and the political economy of technical standards — who sets them, how they entrench power, and what alternative frameworks might look like. Includes analysis of trade agreements, bilateral data deals, and the ITU standardisation process.

Cross-border Data Technical Standards Trade & Digital
04

Digital Public Infrastructure

Assessing the international dimensions of digital public infrastructure (DPI) — the shared digital systems that underpin public service delivery — including their governance, financing, and the role of international organisations in their diffusion.

DPI Governance Digital Development UN Agencies
05

Platform Accountability

Analysing international approaches to holding major digital platforms accountable for their social, economic, and political impacts — including content moderation, competition policy, and the role of international human rights frameworks.

Content Governance Human Rights Competition Policy
06

Political Economy of Standards

Standards are not neutral — they encode values, lock in infrastructure choices, and distribute economic benefits. This strand examines how technical standards are developed, who dominates these processes, and how the outcomes can be made more inclusive and legitimate.

Standards Bodies Geopolitics of Tech Inclusion
Research Approach

All Lab research is independent — conducted without direction from funders, governments, or industry partners.

We use the analytical tools of the social sciences: political economy, international relations, legal analysis, and ethnographic methods.

We prioritise Global South perspectives, both in our research questions and in our research partnerships.

Fellowship Programme

Visiting Fellowship

Visiting Fellowship Programme

The Lab's Visiting Fellowship Programme brings leading researchers — particularly from underrepresented regions — into Geneva's multilateral ecosystem for extended residencies. Fellows engage with the Lab's research agenda and with the diplomatic, organisational, and civil society community that makes Geneva a unique site for digital governance work.

Duration

3 to 6 month residencies, based at the Geneva Graduate Institute

Who Can Apply

Researchers from any discipline with a focus on digital governance, particularly from the Global South

What Fellows Do

Pursue independent research, contribute to Lab projects, and participate in Geneva's digital governance ecosystem

What We Offer

Desk space, institutional affiliation, seminar access, and introductions across Geneva's multilateral network
